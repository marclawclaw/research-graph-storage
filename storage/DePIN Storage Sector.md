## Summary
Decentralised Physical Infrastructure Network (DePIN) storage tokens — primarily [[Filecoin]], [[Arweave]], and [[Storj]] — have seen explosive growth driven by AI data demand, Web3 adoption, and institutional interest in alternatives to centralised cloud. The sector grew from $5.2B market cap in 2024 to over $19B by September 2025 (~270% growth), with storage tokens leading the DePIN category.

## Key Facts

- **Market growth**: DePIN sector grew from ~$5.2B (2024) to $19B+ (Sep 2025) — approximately 270% expansion.
- **September 2024 snapshot**: DePIN tokens at $17B total market cap; Filecoin (FIL) the largest at ~$2.09B, followed by Bittensor (TAO) and Render (RENDER).
- **Messari DePIN projection**: DePIN market estimated to grow to $50B by end of 2024 (optimistic scenario from early-year projection).
- **November 2025 price surge**: FIL +50%, AR +60%, STORJ +20% in first week of November 2025 — investors positioning around blockchain storage as AI storage demand boom mirrors traditional cloud growth.
- **[[Filecoin]] trends**:
  - Q2 2025: Network utilisation rose to 32% despite 13% capacity decline
  - Enterprise datasets grew to 2,491 (Q3 2025), with 925 exceeding 1,000 TiB
  - Strategic pivot from bulk cheap storage to high-value enterprise/research datasets
  - $99M inflows in early 2025 following strategy shift
- **[[Arweave]] trends**:
  - AR token outperformed in the Nov 2025 surge (+60%)
  - Solana validator archiving remains a strong institutional use case
  - [[Irys Bundlr]] evolution signals programmable storage market maturation
- **[[Storj]] trends**:
  - Valdi acquisition (2024) adds GPU compute — positions Storj for AI inference + storage market
  - STORJ gained 20% in Nov 2025 surge
- **Drivers of growth**:
  - AI training datasets require petabyte-scale storage — cloud costs are prohibitive
  - Web3 builders default to decentralised storage for censorship resistance and immutability
  - DeFi and NFT metadata persistence needs beyond what centralised hosts offer
  - Regulatory diversification — data sovereignty concerns driving enterprise interest

> [!analysis] Analyst inference — not verified
> The 270% growth figure appears to be a combined market cap metric and should be treated with caution — crypto market caps inflate on price movements, not just adoption growth. However, the underlying usage metrics (Filecoin dataset counts, Arweave archive size, Storj node count) suggest genuine adoption growth, not pure speculation.

> [!outdated] May be stale — check source date
> The $5.2B → $19B growth figure comes from a March 2026 article. DePIN market caps are highly volatile; the current figure may differ materially.

## How it relates to Logos

- DePIN storage growth validates market demand — Logos Storage is entering a proven sector with real enterprise adoption, not a speculative niche.
- The AI data demand angle is important: if Logos Storage can serve AI training datasets (high-volume, durable, affordable), it taps into one of the fastest-growing storage use cases.
- The convergence of compute + storage (Storj+Valdi, Render Network's GPU+storage model) suggests Logos's long-term stack (Codex + compute primitives) is directionally correct.
- Token-driven storage incentives (FIL, AR, STORJ, SHDW) all face UX friction from token price volatility. Logos should consider fiat-on-ramp or stablecoin payment options from day one.
